### Background

CarePoint Family Clinic is a growing healthcare facility that provides outpatient medical services to patients across several communities.

Over the years, patient information has been collected by multiple receptionists, nurses, and administrative staff using different systems and manual entry processes. As a result, the clinic's patient database contains numerous data quality issues, making it difficult to generate reliable reports and business insights.

The clinic management team has hired you as a Junior Data Analyst to clean and prepare the patient records for analysis.

---

## Business Problem

The clinic is experiencing challenges with:

* Duplicate patient records
* Missing patient information
* Inconsistent data entry formats
* Incorrect demographic information
* Poor-quality contact details
* Inaccurate reporting on patient visits and revenue

Management cannot confidently answer important questions such as:

* How many unique patients visit the clinic?
* What are the most common diagnoses?
* Which doctors see the most patients?
* How much revenue does the clinic generate?
* What is the demographic profile of patients?

Before analysis can be performed, the dataset must be cleaned and standardized.

---

## Your Role

You are a Data Analyst responsible for:

1. Assessing the quality of the dataset.
2. Identifying data issues.
3. Cleaning and standardizing records.
4. Documenting all cleaning decisions.
5. Producing a clean dataset ready for reporting and analysis.

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains synthetic patient records collected from Green Valley Family Clinic.

Typical information includes:

* Patient identifiers
* Demographic information
* Contact details
* Visit information
* Diagnoses
* Assigned doctors
* Payment information

The dataset intentionally contains data quality issues commonly found in real-world healthcare and business datasets.

---

## Project Objectives

### Objective 1: Data Quality Assessment

Perform an initial review of the dataset and identify:

* Missing values
* Duplicate records
* Inconsistent formatting
* Invalid entries
* Data type issues

---

### Objective 2: Data Cleaning

Prepare the dataset by:

* Handling missing values
* Removing duplicates
* Standardizing categorical variables
* Cleaning text fields
* Validating numerical fields
* Standardizing date formats
* Correcting obvious data-entry errors

---

### Objective 3: Data Validation

Ensure:

* Patient IDs are consistent
* Dates are valid
* Contact information is usable
* Categories are standardized
* Numerical values are realistic

---

### Objective 4: Dataset Preparation

Create a final clean dataset suitable for:

* Dashboard development
* Statistical analysis
* Business reporting
* Data visualization

---

## Key Business Questions

After cleaning the dataset, answer the following questions:

### Patient Analysis

* How many unique patients are in the dataset?
* What is the average patient age?
* What is the gender distribution?

### Clinical Analysis

* What are the most common diagnoses?
* Which doctor attended the most patients?
* How many visits occurred each month?

### Financial Analysis

* What is the total revenue generated?
* What is the average payment amount?
* What percentage of payments are still pending?

### Geographic Analysis

* Which city contributes the most patients?
* Which state has the highest number of clinic visits?

## Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful when:

* Missing values have been addressed appropriately.
* Duplicate records have been removed.
* Data formats have been standardized.
* Data quality issues have been documented.
* A clean dataset has been produced.
* Business questions can be answered confidently.
